Granny's Pumpkin Pie

My grandmother's pumpkin pie has been a hit at every Thanksgiving for decades! The pie filling is the original recipe, however the crust was adapted by my mother to make slightly healthier. This is the perfect pie to get you in the mood for autumn. Best served at room temperature with whipped cream!

Ingredients

Pie Crust

  • 1 ¼ cups whole wheat pastry flour
  • 1 Tbsp white sugar
  • ⅛ tsp salt
  • ¼ cup light olive oil
  • ¼ cup milk, fat free or 1% fat

Filling

  • 2 large eggs, beaten
  • 1 cup pumpkin puree
  • 1 ¼ tsp pumpkin spice
  • ½ cup white sugar
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • 1 can (12 oz.) evaporated milk, fat free or 1% fat

Steps

Pie Crust

Skip this section if using a pre-made pie crust.
  1. In a mixing bowl, combine flour, sugar, and salt.
  2. Pour in milk and oil, toss to form a ball. Do not overmix!
  3. Place between 2 sheets of wax paper, lightly floured.
  4. Roll the dough into a circle with an 11.5 inch diameter for a 9 inch deep dish pie pan. (If using an 8 inch pie pan, the diameter should be 10 ¼ inches).
  5. Peel off one sheet of wax paper and carefully invert the dough over pan (wax paper side up). Then peel off the wax paper on top.

Filling

  1. Preheat oven to 425 °F
  2. Beat eggs gently. Stir in all ingredients, then fill into the pie crust.
  3. Bake at 425 °F for 17-18 minutes; turn down the heat to 360 °F and continue to bake for an additional 45 to 50 minutes. (If you're using a small oven, 28-30 minutes).
  4. Cool to room temperature before serving. Store in refrigerator.
